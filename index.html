Welcome!

I earned my PhD in Astronomy from New Mexico State University. My research focuses on galaxy evolution, with special interests in the timescales over which Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN) evolve and impart feedback on their host galaxies and environment, an area known as AGN Archaeology, as well as the cosmic co-evolution of AGN and their host galaxies. I use radio, infrared, and optical observations to identify AGN, constrain the timescales associated with their different feedback (accretion) modes, and study the properties of their host galaxies.

I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher in Extragalactic Astrophysics at Haverford College. In this role, I am assisting both the Roman Science Collaboration and the Galaxy Zoo collaborations to setting up the first Roman Galaxy Zoo project
